Rome Restaurant Guide by Neighborhood: Where Expats Actually Eat

Published: 19 April 2026 Updated: 19 April 2026 16 min read Category: Food & Dining Part of: Rome Food Culture Guide

Every Rome restaurant guide is someone's opinion, including this one. The difference is that this guide is built on DVR community experience -- the collective eating knowledge of expats who have lived in Rome for years and have eaten their way through these restaurants repeatedly, not once. Restaurants are visited multiple times, assessed for consistency, and updated when quality changes. No restaurant on this list paid to appear here.

The guide is organized by neighborhood because location is the most practical organizing principle for residents. Tourist-area restaurants are largely excluded -- not because they are all bad (some are excellent) but because this guide is primarily for people building a food life in Rome rather than ticking sights.

How to use this guide: Reservations are increasingly necessary at Rome's better restaurants, especially on weekends. Call ahead (Italian phone manners: introduce yourself, name the date and time, number of people, and "C'è posto?" -- is there space?). Many restaurants do not take reservations and operate on a walk-in queue; arrive early (6:45-7:15pm) or late (9pm+) to minimize waiting.

Testaccio: Rome's Food Neighborhood

Testaccio consistently produces Rome's most reliable restaurant experiences because its clientele is demanding and local. The neighborhood's chefs have been cooking Roman food for Romans for generations and have developed a precision that tourist-area restaurants rarely need. These are the restaurants where DVR members eat most frequently.

Osteria Flavio al Velavevodetto

Via Monte Testimonio 98, Testaccio Tel: +39 06 574 4194 Lunch & dinner Tue-Sun Reservation: Recommended for dinner

What to order: Cacio e pepe (the standard reference), carciofi alla romana (winter-spring), and the house secondo -- whatever the daily special is will be better than the fixed menu items.

Price: EUR 28-40 for two courses with house wine.

"This is where I send every person who asks me where to eat in Rome. The cacio e pepe is not just good -- it is the thing I compare everything else to. The room is comfortable, the service is warm, and the bill is honest." -- James Holbrook, DVR Testaccio member

Da Remo

Piazza di Santa Maria Liberatrice 44, Testaccio Dinner Mon-Sat (closed Sunday) No reservations; queue or arrive by 7pm

What to order: Carbonara, the thin Roman pizza (the house specialty), supplì as starter. This is primarily a pizza restaurant -- the best thin-crust Roman pizza in the neighborhood. The carbonara is a notable secondhighlight.

Price: EUR 15-25 per person with wine. One of Rome's best value dinners.

"Da Remo is not atmospheric. The room is bright, loud, and crowded. The pizza is extraordinary. The carbonara is extraordinary. The bill is reasonable. This is the trade-off Testaccio makes and I find it excellent." -- Andreas Mueller, DVR Ostiense member

Checchino dal 1887

Via di Monte Testaccio 30, Testaccio Tue-Sat lunch & dinner; closed August Reservation: Required

What to order: Coda alla vaccinara (the definitive version), rigatoni con pajata (for the adventurous), any of the quinto quarto preparations. This is Rome's historic reference for offal cooking.

Price: EUR 45-70 per person. The most expensive restaurant on this list.

"Checchino is not cheap, but it is a living archive of Roman culinary tradition. The family has been serving the same dishes since 1887 using recipes passed through generations. For quinto quarto, there is no equal."

Trastevere: Quality Among the Tourists

Trastevere has excellent restaurants but they require knowing which to choose. The ratio of tourist-adjusted to genuinely good is less favorable here than in Testaccio, but the best Trastevere restaurants are among Rome's finest for a combination of food quality, atmosphere, and expat accessibility.

Da Enzo al 29

Via dei Vascellari 29, Trastevere Lunch & dinner Mon-Sat Reservation: Essential; book 1-2 weeks ahead

What to order: Whatever the daily specials are, plus gricia or carbonara from the fixed menu. The offal dishes when available (Thursday tripe).

Price: EUR 30-45 per person with wine. Worth every euro.

"Enzo has been the most recommended restaurant in the DVR community for three consecutive years. The reason is consistency: every visit is as good as the last. In a city where quality can swing with staff changes, this reliability is rare and valuable."

Tonnarello

Via della Paglia 1, Trastevere Lunch & dinner daily Reservation: Recommended evenings

What to order: Cacio e pepe (the house specialty), the seasonal vegetable antipasti in winter and spring, house wine carafe.

Price: EUR 25-38 per person with wine.

"Tonnarello is in the heart of tourist Trastevere but maintains quality through sheer reputation and neighborhood loyalty. The cacio e pepe is genuinely excellent. The outdoor terrace in summer is one of the best places to eat in Rome."

Prati: Reliable Quality for Local Life

Osteria dell'Angelo

Via Baldo degli Ubaldi 9, Prati Lunch Mon-Fri; Dinner Mon-Sat Fixed-price dinner: EUR 25 per person all-in

What to order: The fixed-price dinner menu -- no choices, you eat what the kitchen is making tonight. Typically includes antipasto, primo (often gricia or amatriciana), secondo (often lamb), and dessert.

Price: EUR 25 fixed dinner price is one of Rome's best value deals. Include wine for EUR 30-35 total.

"Osteria dell'Angelo is where I take people who think Rome restaurants are expensive. Twenty-five euros for a complete Roman dinner that is genuinely good food and includes everything except your wine? It exists. It is in Prati. Book it." -- Marie Chen, DVR Prati member

Il Sorpasso

Via Properzio 31, Prati All day: 8am-midnight No reservation; walk-in

What to order: Natural wine by the glass, small plates at aperitivo, and their excellent weekend brunch. Transitional food -- between bar and restaurant -- that represents Prati's more contemporary food scene.

Price: EUR 6-12 per glass wine; EUR 18-28 for a light meal.

"Il Sorpasso is where Prati's international community actually meets. It operates as an all-day bar, wine shop, and restaurant simultaneously. The wine program is serious. The aperitivo hour is Rome's best neighborhood social event."

Ostiense & Beyond: Neighborhood Favorites

Roscioli Salumeria con Cucina

Via dei Giubbonari 21 (near Campo de' Fiori) Mon-Sat: 9am-midnight Reservation: Required for dinner

What to order: Carbonara (one of Rome's best), the charcuterie selection from the salumeria counter, and any of the daily fish specials. The wine list is extraordinary by Roman standards.

Price: EUR 40-60 per person with wine. Expensive by Roman standards but worth it for a special occasion.

"Roscioli is Rome's best all-round food establishment: salumeria, restaurant, wine bar, and bakery under one roof. For a special dinner, carbonara at Roscioli is the definitive version of the definitive Roman dish."

The Tourist Trap Warning Signs

Several reliable signals indicate that a restaurant is operating for tourists rather than residents:

Photos of food on the menu (almost universal indicator). Menus in five languages with no Italian-only version. "No cover charge" prominently advertised (this means they're compensating elsewhere in the pricing). Multilingual hosts actively recruiting from the pavement. Prices that don't match the neighborhood's general level. Tables with tourists only, no Italians in evidence. Menus that don't change with the season.

None of these are instant disqualifiers -- occasional tourist restaurants are decent -- but in combination they reliably predict an adjusted experience. The food may be technically adequate but it is rarely excellent, and it is priced to absorb tourist margins rather than feed locals.
