Amalfi Coast Day Trip from Rome: Positano & Amalfi

Published April 19, 2026 | Updated April 2026 | Reading time: 8 minutes

The Reality

The Amalfi Coast is not a relaxing day trip. It's a 14-hour endurance test that rewards you with some of the most dramatic scenery in Southern Italy. Positano is a vertical village where buildings stack on top of each other like a Jenga tower, descending toward turquoise water. Amalfi is a coastal town with a thousand-year history and a cathedral that survived Crusades and bombs. The ferries cut through Mediterranean blue. The seafood pasta is the best you'll eat all year.

This is the day trip you'll talk about for months.

Getting There: Roma Termini to Salerno

Frecciarossa Train

  • Departure: Roma Termini 07:00 (or 07:30 later option)
  • Arrival: Salerno Centrale 09:45 (or 10:15)
  • Journey time: 2 hours 45 minutes
  • Cost: EUR 25-40 if booked 2-3 weeks ahead, EUR 45-60 walk-up or last-minute
  • Booking: Go to trenitalia.com 3 weeks before trip. Search "Roma Termini" to "Salerno" for your date, 07:00 departure. Book both outbound and return together (round-trip discount). Alternatively, walk up to Termini ticket window 30 minutes before departure.
  • Return: Salerno to Roma 18:00-18:30 departure (book same time as outbound)

Book Early for Price Lock

Frecciarossa fares to Salerno are the biggest expense. Booking 3 weeks ahead saves EUR 20-30 per person. Set a calendar reminder and book immediately when the date window opens (trenitalia.com releases fares 3 months in advance).

Salerno Station to Amalfi

Salerno is the gateway to the Amalfi Coast. From the station, you need to get to the port/ferry terminal (for ferry) or bus station (for bus). Both are about 5-10 minutes walk from the train station.

Options: Bus vs Ferry to Amalfi

  • Ferry (recommended): Faster (1 hour vs 1.5 hours), more scenic (sea views), arrives directly in Amalfi harbor. Cost EUR 12 (vs EUR 2.50 bus). Departs Salerno 10:00 and 11:00 hourly. Buy ticket at ferry office near harbor.
  • Bus (cheaper): SITA bus service, EUR 2.50, takes 1.5 hours (winding coastal road), departs Salerno bus station hourly (10:00, 10:30, 11:00, etc.). Takes a scenic road through the coast (worth seeing) but slower.
  • Recommendation: Take ferry for speed and views. That extra EUR 10 is worth the time saved.

Amalfi: First Stop (1 hour)

Amalfi is the "capital" of the Amalfi Coast. A medieval trading republic, it's been a coastal town for a thousand years. The Cathedral of Sant'Andrea (Duomo) is the main attraction -- a stunning structure with a 13th-century bronze door (cast in Constantinople) and the relics of Saint Andrew inside.

What to See (1 hour)

  • Cathedral of Sant'Andrea: Free entry (donations welcome), 20-30 minutes inside. Ornate interior, bronze doors, reliquaries. The exterior is white and black marble, dramatic.
  • Piazza Duomo: The main square with lemon trees (Amalfi lemons are famous). Sit, rest, drink lemon water. 15 minutes.
  • Topical Paper Museum: EUR 5, optional. Amalfi invented paper manufacturing in medieval times. Small museum showing traditional paper making. Skip if short on time.
  • Wandering the town: Narrow streets, boutique shops, small cafes. 15-20 minutes of aimless wandering rewarded with hidden courtyards.

Ferry/Bus to Positano (30 min ferry or 1 hour bus)

Positano is 5km further along the coast from Amalfi. It's accessible by ferry (faster, scenic) or bus (slower, cheaper, winding road). Ferry departs Amalfi 12:00, 12:30, 13:00, etc. Cost EUR 8 (30 minutes). Bus departs hourly, EUR 2.50, 1 hour.

Recommendation: Take the ferry. The 30-minute ride is spectacular -- you're seeing the coast the way Amalfi was originally connected to the world (by sea).

Positano: The Main Event (3-4 hours)

Positano is the postcard village of the Amalfi Coast. A vertical town where pastel-colored buildings stack on cliffsides, descending toward turquoise water. Population 3,000, but feels like a private village. This is where you spend your afternoon.

Positano Exploration (1 hour)

  • The village itself: Walk the switchback streets from top to bottom. Stop at viewpoints. It's all about the atmosphere and photos. 45 minutes of wandering.
  • Spiaggia Grande (main beach): At the bottom, free access. Lounge chairs EUR 5-10. Umbrella EUR 5. You can stay for 1-2 hours swimming and resting.
  • Boutique shopping: Designer shops line the streets (Prada, Gucci, local brands). Window shop or buy something overpriced but memorable.
  • Photography: Every corner is a photo. Budget extra time for this.

Lunch in Positano (1.5-2 hours)

Restaurant Options

  • La Tagliata: Upscale, on the hill overlooking the coast. Reservations essential (+39 089 875 040). Pasta with seafood, fresh vegetables from their garden. EUR 30-45 per person. Possibly the best meal you'll eat in 2026. Worth the reservation.
  • Ristorante Max: Seafood, waterfront, good service. EUR 25-35 per person. Less famous than Tagliata but excellent. Easier to walk in without reservation.
  • Casual lunch: Panini or pizza from street vendors (EUR 8-12). Sit on the beach and eat. Not fancy but functional and cheaper.

The Seafood Pasta Rule

Order pasta with seafood (pasta ai frutti di mare) or spaghetti with clams (spaghetti alle vongole). It's what this coast is famous for. The pasta is handmade, the seafood is caught that morning, the sauce is white wine and garlic. It's life-changing.

Beach Time (1-2 hours)

After lunch, head to Spiaggia Grande beach. Swim for 20-30 minutes (water is 20-24C in summer, wettable but not warm). Dry off, rest on the beach or at a cafe. The afternoon light is golden. Relax for 1 hour.

Budget: Lounge chair EUR 5-10 (optional, you can sit on the sand), umbrella EUR 5 (recommended), drink EUR 4-6.

Alternative: Path of the Gods Hike

The Sentiero degli Dei (Path of the Gods) is a 12km hiking trail from Praiano (next village) to Positano, featuring dramatic coastal views. If you skip lunch/beach and do the hike instead: 4-5 hours of hiking, dramatic photos, arrive at Positano tired but exhilarated. Only do this if you're fit and have 6+ hours to dedicate.

Return to Salerno (Ferry or Bus)

Ferry departs Positano around 16:00-16:45 (check schedule when you arrive), arrives Salerno 17:00-17:45 (1 hour). Bus departs similar times, takes 1.5 hours. Either way, you need to be back in Salerno by 17:45 to catch the 18:00-18:30 Frecciarossa to Rome.

Timing is Tight

You have 1 hour buffer between arriving Salerno and the 18:00 train departure. If ferry/bus is delayed, you'll miss your train. Confirm return ferry/bus times when you arrive in Positano morning. Have a backup plan (later train at 19:00-20:00).

Return Train to Rome

Frecciarossa departs Salerno around 18:00-18:30, arrives Roma Termini 20:45-21:15. You'll have gone 14 hours door-to-door. You'll be exhausted. You'll be happy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you do the Amalfi Coast in a day from Rome?
Yes, barely. Total travel time is 5.5-6 hours round trip. You'll have 4-5 hours at the coast (1 hour Amalfi, 3-4 hours Positano). It's exhausting but rewarding. Most people say it was worth it.
Is the ferry worth the extra cost?
Yes. Ferry is 30 minutes faster than bus and far more scenic. You pay EUR 10 extra for saves 30 minutes and unforgettable views. Worth it given the tight schedule.
Can you swim in the Amalfi Coast in summer?
Yes. Water temperature is 20-24C in July-August (warmer than you'd expect). It's not tropical but swimmable. Bring swimwear and plan 30-45 minutes for swimming.
What is the Path of the Gods hike?
Sentiero degli Dei is a 12km coastal hiking trail from Praiano to Positano with dramatic cliff views. Takes 4-5 hours. Doable on a day trip but requires skipping lunch and beach time. Only do if you're fit.
Where should you eat on the Amalfi Coast?
La Tagliata in Positano is exceptional (reservations essential, EUR 30-40). Ristorante Max is easier walk-in access (EUR 25-35). For budget, casual panini vendors (EUR 8-12). Avoid tourist traps directly on the main beach (overpriced).
What is the best time to visit the Amalfi Coast?
May-June and September-October. Weather is warm, water is 20-24C, crowds are moderate. Avoid July-August (extreme heat 35C+, extreme crowds, prices triple). Winter is quiet but water is 15-17C (cold).
Should you stay overnight instead of doing a day trip?
If your budget allows, yes. One night in Positano lets you explore at a sane pace, enjoy dinner without rushing, swim without watching the clock, watch the sunset. Cost is EUR 80-200+ per night. Day trip is budget option (spend only EUR 100-165 total).
What should you bring to the Amalfi Coast?
Swimwear (or buy sarong at destination), sunscreen (non-negotiable -- sea reflects sun intensely), hat, water bottle, light layers, flip-flops, sea bag for wet items, camera. The sun is intense here.

Final Thoughts

The Amalfi Coast day trip is the most exhausting of the five day trips in this guide. But it's also the most dramatic. You'll see views that appear in travel magazines. You'll eat pasta that tastes like you've never eaten before. You'll swim in Mediterranean water. You'll remember this day for decades.
