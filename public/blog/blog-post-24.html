ATAC Network Overview

Rome's ATAC (Azienda dei Trasporti Autoferrotranviari del Comune di Roma) operates 330+ bus routes and 6 tram lines covering the entire city. Buses reach neighborhoods the Metro misses entirely -- Trastevere, Campo de' Fiori, Piazza Navona, and the Jewish Ghetto have no metro stations. The downside: buses are slower, prone to delay (15--30 minutes is routine on tourist corridors), and pickpocket-friendly due to crowding.

Quick Facts:
  • 330+ bus routes, 6 tram lines
  • Single ride: EUR 1.50 (100 minute ticket on bus/tram, can transfer to metro)
  • Operating hours: 5:30am--11:30pm, Night buses (N-lines) midnight--5:30am
  • Frequency: 10--15 min on main routes, 20--30min on residential routes
  • Delays: 15--30min routine on tourist routes, less on local lines
  • Validation: Must validate ticket in yellow readers on board. Fine EUR 100+ if caught without validation.

Key Tourist Routes

Rome's 330 bus routes can be overwhelming. Here are the essential lines for tourists and visitors:

Bus 40 & 64: Termini to Vatican (WARNING: High Pickpocket Risk)

The main tourist route connects Termini station to St. Peter's Basilica/Vatican, passing through the Historic Center. Route covers Termini -- Via Nazionale -- Largo Magnanapoli -- Pantheon -- Navona -- Campo de' Fiori -- Bridge over Tiber -- Vatican. Single journey 30--40 minutes depending on traffic.

Important: Bus 40/64 has the highest pickpocket concentration in Rome, especially 8--10am and 4--7pm. Stand near the driver if possible, keep your bag in front, and don't use your phone. This route is useful but genuinely risky. Alternative: Take Line A metro to Ottaviano instead, it's faster and safer.

Bus 23: Lungotevere (Tiber Scenic Route)

Scenic route along the Tiber River from Testaccio (south) through the city center to Prati (near Vatican). Less crowded than Bus 40/64. Good for sightseeing or exploring neighborhoods near the river. 45--60 min end-to-end.

Bus H: Termini to Ostiense (via Trastevere)

Fast express route from Termini through Trastevere to Ostiense district (south). Useful for Trastevere access without relying on slower buses. 25--30 min.

Bus 590: Metro Parallel Route

Follows the same route as Line A metro (Termini -- Barberini -- Trevi -- Spanish Steps -- Flaminio -- Vatican area). Same EUR 1.50 ticket but on the surface, slower (40--50 min), but useful if metro is crowded or you prefer street-level views.

Tram 8: Largo Argentina to Trastevere

Dedicated tram track from Largo Argentina (Historic Center) into Trastevere, more reliable than buses. 15--20 min. Recommended over buses for Trastevere access.

Tram 2 & 3: Residential & Trastevere

Tram 2 connects Flaminio to northern areas. Tram 3 serves Trastevere's edges. Less tourist-focused, good for neighborhood exploration.

Tram Lines: The Reliable Alternative

Rome's tram system (6 lines) is more reliable than buses because trams run on dedicated tracks, unaffected by car traffic. If you're going to Trastevere or other neighborhoods served by trams, choose trams over buses -- you'll arrive roughly on time.

Main Tram Lines:

  • Tram 2: Flaminio to Mancini area, useful for northern neighborhoods
  • Tram 3: Trastevere to Piazza Thorvaldsen, neighborhood exploration
  • Tram 8: Largo Argentina to Trastevere via Santo Spirito. Best tram line for tourists. 15--20 min. Always on time.
  • Tram 19: Gerani to Risorgimento (Vatican area), less touristy
Pro Tip: If visiting Trastevere, take Tram 8 from Largo Argentina. It's faster, more reliable, and less crowded than buses. Never wait for a bus when a tram does the same route -- trams always beat buses.

Night Buses (Notturno N-Lines)

When the Metro closes at 11:30pm (1:30am Fri-Sat), Rome's Night Bus system takes over. Routes prefixed "N" (N1, N2, N3, N4, N5) operate midnight to 5:30am on main corridors. They're less frequent (20--30 min intervals) and stop at major hubs, not residential stops.

Avoid Night Buses If Possible: Night buses are slower, less frequent, and you'll wait at isolated stops alone. After midnight, use taxis or Uber instead -- it's safer and more reliable. Only use night buses if traveling with others or from well-lit main-road stops.

Validation, Tickets & Fares

Validation: Non-Negotiable

Bus and tram tickets must be validated in the yellow electronic readers on board before or immediately after boarding. Tap or insert your card/ticket and wait for the beep. Plain-clothes transit inspectors board frequently and fine EUR 100+ for invalid tickets. This rule is enforced -- no exceptions, no negotiations.

Ticket Types

Ticket Type Price (EUR) Valid For
Single Ride 1.50 100 min on bus/tram (can transfer between buses/trams)
24-Hour Pass 7.00 Unlimited metro, bus, tram for 24 hours
48-Hour Pass 12.50 Unlimited for 48 hours (best value for 2-day trips)
Weekly Pass 24.00 7 days unlimited (good for expats)

Apps & Real-Time Tracking

Google Maps (Most Reliable)

Google Maps is your best tool for Rome bus navigation. Search your route, select transit, and you'll see all bus/tram/metro options with real-time delays and estimated arrival times. Download the Rome offline map first. The app pulls live ATAC data and is more accurate than the ATAC app itself.

Citymapper

Beautiful app popular with Rome expats. Shows all transit options, real-time delays, and crowding predictions. Slightly less detailed than Google Maps but more intuitive. Excellent for planning multi-leg journeys.

ATAC Official App

Not recommended. The official ATAC app is slow, unreliable, and crashes frequently. Use Google Maps instead -- it has better real-time data. Roman residents still use ATAC from habit, but tourists and expats are better served by Google Maps or Citymapper.

Safety & Pickpocket Hotspots

Highest Risk Routes

  • Bus 40/64: Termini to Vatican. Peak risk 8--10am and 4--7pm. Professional pickpocket gangs work crowded buses. Stand near driver, keep bag in front, don't use phone.
  • Bus 23: Tiber route, moderate risk due to tourist crowds
  • Tram 8: Generally safer than buses, less crowded, more reliable. Lower pickpocket risk.

General Safety Rules for Buses

  • Keep your bag in front, not on your back
  • Avoid using your phone on crowded buses (easy target)
  • Don't display expensive watches, jewelry, or cameras
  • Stand near the door or driver if possible, not in the middle of a crowded car
  • Be aware of people standing very close to you -- pickpockets often work in pairs

Practical Bus Tips

Reading Bus Stops

Bus stops (fermata) display route numbers, destinations, and sometimes timetables. Stop names change frequently due to city construction. Use Google Maps to confirm your stop -- the app will tell you exactly when to exit.

Signaling the Driver

Stand on the pavement edge or at the marked stop, and signal the driver with a wave or raised hand. On busy routes, drivers expect to stop; on less-busy routes, signal clearly. Buses don't stop if you're not visible or there are no passengers getting off.

Bus Delays Are Routine

Rome buses are chronically late. 15--30 minutes delay on tourist routes (Bus 40/64, Bus 23) is normal. Rush hour (7:30--9am, 5--7:30pm) is worst. If you're on a schedule, take the metro instead. If using buses, assume worst case and build in time.

Getting Off

Press the stop-request button (usually red) as the bus approaches your destination. The bus will beep and the driver will pull over. If you don't request, the driver won't stop -- buses don't stop at every stop, only where passengers request.

Trastevere Instead of Bus Chaos?

If heading to Trastevere, skip buses and take Tram 8 from Largo Argentina. It's faster, more reliable, and arrives on time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why are Rome buses always late? +

Rome buses operate in car-congested city streets, especially on tourist corridors like Bus 40/64 to Vatican. Buses aren't on dedicated tracks (unlike trams), so they're stuck in traffic. 15--30 minute delays are standard. If you're on a tight schedule, take the metro instead. Or use Google Maps for real-time tracking -- at least you'll know how late you'll actually be.

Should I take Bus 40/64 to the Vatican despite pickpocket warnings? +

It's risky but possible if you're careful. Keep your bag in front, don't use your phone, and avoid traveling 8--10am or 4--7pm. Better option: Take Line A metro to Ottaviano station instead -- it's faster (25 min vs. 40 min), safer, and more comfortable. The metro is better value for money.

What's the difference between a bus ticket and a metro ticket? +

They're the same ticket. EUR 1.50 is valid for 100 minutes on buses/trams OR one metro ride. If you buy a single ticket for the metro, you can't use it on a bus. But multi-pass tickets (24hr, 48hr, weekly) work on all modes (metro, bus, tram).

How do I find my bus stop and know when to get off? +

Use Google Maps: input your destination, select the transit route, and the app will show your stop and alert you when to get off. Download the Rome map offline first. Don't rely on trying to read street signs -- just follow the app's instructions. Press the stop button a few stops before your destination to signal the driver.

Are trams safer than buses in Rome? +

Yes. Trams run on dedicated tracks, arrive on schedule, and are less crowded. They're genuinely better for safety and reliability. If your destination has tram access (like Trastevere via Tram 8), always choose the tram over the bus. Trams beat buses on every metric.

What happens if I forget to validate my ticket? +

Transit inspectors will fine you EUR 100+ on the spot. They board buses and trams regularly, especially on touristy routes. Validation takes 2 seconds -- you must do it before or right after boarding. No exceptions, no negotiation. Every bus/tram has yellow validators on board or near the door.

Are night buses safe after midnight? +

Night buses are less safe than daytime buses. They're less frequent (20--30 min), make fewer stops, and you'll often wait alone at isolated bus stops. Use them only if traveling with others or from well-lit main-road stops. After midnight, taxis or Uber are safer and more reliable options.
