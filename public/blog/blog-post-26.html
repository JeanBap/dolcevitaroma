Official Taxis: How to Identify & Rates

Rome's official taxi system is straightforward if you know the rules. White official taxis operate on fixed tariffs set by the city. Unofficial taxis and fake drivers exist (especially at airports and Termini station), but they're rare if you know what to look for. Official taxis are regulated, safer, and always metered.

How to Identify an Official Taxi

  • White body with official taxi license number displayed on the door (usually a blue and yellow plaque with a number)
  • Visible meter (tassametro) on the dashboard, running and displaying the current fare
  • ID card visible with driver name and license number
  • Official Rome taxi company name on the door (e.g., Roma Taxi, 3570, 8888)
AVOID Fake Taxis: Never accept a ride from a driver who approaches you unsolicited at Termini, airports, or tourist areas. Never enter a car that doesn't have the official white color and license plate. Unofficial taxis will quote inflated prices. Always go to an official taxi rank (posteggio) or call a licensed taxi company via app.

Metered Fares & Tariffs

Official Rome taxis use metered rates set by city regulation. Rates vary by time of day and day of week. You can estimate your fare, but the meter is the final authority.

Time Period Starting Fare (EUR) Per Kilometer (EUR)
Day (7am--10pm) 3.50 1.10
Night (10pm--7am) 6.50 1.30
Sundays & Holidays 5.50 1.30

Additional Charges

  • EUR 1 per large bag (luggage)
  • EUR 1 if traveling with pets or animals
  • EUR 3 radio dispatch surcharge (if driver dispatches via radio rather than you hailing directly)

Example: A 5km ride during the day (7am--10pm) would be: EUR 3.50 (start) + (5km × EUR 1.10) = EUR 8.50. Add EUR 1 per bag if you have luggage. Total: approx. EUR 9.50--10.50 depending on traffic stops.

Fixed Fares & Taxi Ranks

Fixed Fares by Law

Two routes have legally fixed fares set by Rome's city government:

  • Fiumicino Airport to City Center (any hotel/location): EUR 55 (day or night, no surges, fixed by law)
  • Ciampino Airport to City Center: EUR 31 (fixed by law)

Critical: Drivers must offer the fixed fare upfront. If a taxi driver at Fiumicino doesn't mention EUR 55 fixed fare, ask "What is the fixed fare to the city?" If he says there isn't one or quotes higher, insist on the correct fare. It's a tourist protection law.

Main Taxi Ranks (Posteggio Taxi)

Official taxi ranks where you can queue for the first available licensed taxi:

  • Roma Termini (Main Station): Multiple ranks outside the station. Eastern exit (via Giolitti) has the largest rank. Busy, tourist-heavy, long waits during peak hours.
  • Piazza Venezia: Central location, moderate wait times
  • Piazza Navona: Tourist area, longer waits
  • Trevi Fountain Area: Extremely touristy, very long waits (avoid)
  • Piazza del Popolo: Northern edge, shorter waits
  • Campo de' Fiori: Morning/early afternoon only
  • Fiumicino & Ciampino Airports: Dedicated taxi areas with fixed fares

Pro Tip: If stuck in a long queue at Termini, walk 5 minutes away to a quieter area and hail a passing taxi with the light on (available). You'll find one faster.

Taxi Apps: Italo Taxi & FREE NOW

Italo Taxi / IT Taxi (Rome's Official App)

The official Rome taxi app. You request a ride, the app shows an estimated fixed price (calculated from metered rates), you get matched with a nearby taxi, and you pay via app. No surprises, no negotiation, professional drivers.

Advantages:

  • Price shown before booking -- no meter surprises
  • GPS tracking of your driver
  • Licensed, official Rome taxis only
  • No surge pricing

Disadvantages:

  • App sometimes slow or glitchy
  • Prices slightly higher than street hailing (includes dispatch surcharge)

FREE NOW (Formerly MyTaxi)

Books official licensed Rome taxis via app. Similar to Italo Taxi -- shows estimated price before booking, matches you with a nearby taxi, no surge pricing. Many locals prefer FREE NOW over Italo Taxi for reliability.

Advantages:

  • Very reliable, same-day support
  • Only official taxis, no Uber drivers
  • Estimated price shown upfront
  • No surge pricing

Uber: Cost Comparison & Surge Pricing

Uber operates in Rome (UberX and Uber Comfort available). It's useful for airport rides with fixed pricing and credit card pre-payment, but not always cheaper than taxis.

Uber Pricing Model

  • Surge pricing: Prices increase during demand peaks (6--8pm rush hour, late night after midnight, weekends). A EUR 10 standard ride becomes EUR 15--20+ during surge.
  • Distance + time: Calculated by Uber's algorithm, not city meters. Results vary based on route selected by app.
  • Airport rides: Fixed prices shown upfront (e.g., Fiumicino to central Rome EUR 55--75 depending on surge). Often the same as official taxi fares.

When Uber Wins

  • Airport transfers with app booking: Fixed price, credit card already saved, no cash needed
  • When taxis are on strike: Italian taxi drivers strike several times per year. On strike days, use Uber as backup.
  • Early morning (before rush hour): No surge, prices are standard or lower
  • If you don't have euros: Uber accepts credit cards; some taxis are cash-only

When Taxis Win

  • During surge pricing: Taxis use fixed metered rates, never surge. A EUR 10 ride stays EUR 10 even at midnight.
  • Late night after 11pm: Uber surges heavily, taxis are steady night rates
  • Ciampino Airport: Taxi fixed fare EUR 31 vs. Uber EUR 30--50. Taxi is nearly always cheaper.
  • Heavy luggage: Taxi luggage charge (EUR 1 per bag) is minor. Uber's time/distance multiplier gets expensive with multiple luggage loads.

Airport Transfers: Fiumicino & Ciampino

Fiumicino Airport (FCO -- Main International)

No Metro Connection. Fiumicino is served by train and taxis/Uber only.

Method Cost (EUR) Duration Notes
Taxi (Fixed Fare) 55 45--60 min Official white taxi, fixed by law, metered after if you go farther
Uber 55--75 45--60 min Fixed price shown before booking, surge pricing may apply
Leonardo Express Train 14 32 min Direct to Termini station, runs every 30 min, no luggage handling

Recommendation: Taxi or Uber if you have luggage or prefer door-to-door. Leonardo Express train if traveling light and want the cheapest option.

Ciampino Airport (CIA -- Budget Airlines)

No Metro Connection. Served by buses and taxis only.

  • Taxi (Fixed Fare): EUR 31
  • ATAC Bus 590 & others: EUR 1.50, 60--90 min, multiple stops
  • Uber: EUR 30--50 (often more than taxi)

Recommendation: Taxi at EUR 31 is nearly always the best value. Uber is often more expensive. Buses are cheapest but slow.

Scams & How to Avoid Them

Fake Taxi Scams

The Setup: Unlicensed drivers approach tourists at Termini, Fiumicino, or tourist areas. They claim to be taxis, quote inflated flat rates (EUR 20--50 for short rides), and drive you around inefficiently to maximize the bill.

How to Avoid:

  • Never accept rides from drivers who approach you unsolicited
  • Verify white official taxi color and license plate before entering
  • Go to official taxi rank (posteggio) at Termini, not random cars outside
  • Use Italo Taxi or FREE NOW app to book official taxis
  • At airports, ask a staff member where the official taxi rank is

Airport Fixed Fare Denial

The Scam: Taxi driver at Fiumicino claims there is no fixed fare and quotes EUR 60, EUR 70, or more. You don't know the fixed fare exists, so you pay.

How to Avoid:

  • Know the fixed fares: Fiumicino EUR 55, Ciampino EUR 31
  • When calling taxi or before getting in, ask: "What is the fixed tariff to the city center?"
  • If driver says "no fixed fare" or quotes higher, refuse and walk to official rank
  • Use Uber with its fixed price shown upfront

Meter Tampering (Rare But Exists)

Some dishonest drivers have rigged meters that run faster than actual distance. This is rare and Rome's meters are official, but:

  • Watch the meter display during your ride
  • If it jumps or runs unusually fast, speak up and request to stop
  • Get a receipt (ricevuta) with the final fare printed
  • Report suspicious behavior to your hotel or police

Alternative Options: E-Scooters & E-Bikes

Electric Scooters (Lime, Bird, Tier, Helbiz)

E-scooter companies operate in Rome. Prices: EUR 1 unlock + EUR 0.25 per minute. Useful for 1--3km hops but:

  • Cannot enter ZTL (Limited Traffic Zone) in centro storico
  • Watch for reckless riders on sidewalks
  • Parking regulations exist (must park in designated areas)
  • Popular with young Romans, less with tourists

E-Bikes & Bikeshare

JUMP and Bici Roma offer bike sharing. 24-hour pass EUR 13. Good for fitness-minded travelers, but Rome's traffic is intense. Most visitors stick with taxis, metro, or walking.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to use unofficial taxis in Rome? +

No. Unofficial taxis are unmetered, unregulated, and driven by unlicensed drivers. You'll overpay and potentially risk your safety. Always use official white taxis with license plates, taxi apps (Italo Taxi, FREE NOW), or Uber. The extra few euros are worth the safety and fair pricing.

How much does a typical taxi ride in Rome cost? +

Short ride (3--5km, e.g., Termini to Colosseum): EUR 10--15. Medium ride (5--10km, e.g., Termini to Vatican): EUR 15--25. Long ride (10km+): EUR 25+. Always expect traffic delays, especially 7:30--9am and 5:30--7pm. Night rates (10pm--7am) are 25% higher starting fare.

Should I tip taxi drivers in Rome? +

Tipping is not obligatory in Rome. Rounding up to the nearest EUR 5 or EUR 10 is appreciated if the driver was helpful or the ride was long. If using a taxi app, you can add a tip in the app. Cash tips are less common but welcome. Most tourists don't tip and drivers don't expect it.

Can I negotiate taxi fares in Rome? +

No. Official taxis use metered rates set by city law -- there's nothing to negotiate. If a driver quotes a flat rate instead of turning on the meter, refuse and find another taxi. Apps (Italo Taxi, FREE NOW) show fixed prices upfront. Never negotiate with unofficial "taxis" that approach you.

What's the best app for booking taxis in Rome? +

Italo Taxi (official Rome app) and FREE NOW are both reliable. Italo Taxi is Rome-specific and often used by locals. FREE NOW is part of an international network and has strong customer support. Both book official licensed taxis, show fixed prices upfront, and have no surge pricing. Choose whichever you download first -- both work well.

Do Roman taxi drivers accept credit cards? +

Most do, but cash is still common. Larger taxi companies often have card readers. If you're booking via app (Italo Taxi, FREE NOW, Uber), payment is automatic. If hailing a street taxi, ask "accettate carte?" (do you accept cards?) before getting in. Always have some euros cash as backup.

What should I do if a taxi driver overcharges me? +

Get a receipt (ricevuta), note the taxi number and driver name, and report it to Rome's taxi authority or your hotel. If you booked via app (Italo Taxi, FREE NOW, Uber), disputes are handled through the app. Most drivers won't overcharge because the meter is official and passengers can report violations. Stay calm and get documentation if you believe you're being cheated.

Are taxis or Uber better for airport transfers? +

For Fiumicino: Nearly equivalent. Taxi fixed fare EUR 55 vs. Uber EUR 55--75 (surge may apply). Taxi is reliable, Uber is convenient if surge is low. For Ciampino: Taxi wins (EUR 31 fixed vs. Uber EUR 30--50). If you don't speak Italian and want credit card payment, use Uber. If you want to avoid surge pricing, use taxi fixed fares.
